Elisa Beshero-Bondar is principle investigator and technical coordinator of the Digital Mitford project. She is Associate Professor of English and directs the Center for the Digital Text at Pitt-Greensburg.
Mike Bolam heads the Metadata and Discovery Unit of the University of Pittsburgh University Library System. He is leading our Data Cleaning workshop.
Greg Bondar is the Manuscript Archaeologist on the Digital Mitford project. He has photographed over 800 of Mitford’s letters in the Reading Central Library, the John Rylands Library in Manchester, and elsewhere. He maintains the Digital Mitford project’s database documenting over 2700 individual letters and manuscripts. His research involves archaeological excavations at Tell Timai in Egypt, San Jose de Moro in Peru, and analyzing stone tools with Penn State’s nuclear reactor. While he has only been involved with Digital Humanities applications since 2013, he spent many years marking up ethnographic data in the mid-1990s.
Aaron Brenner is Associate University Librarian for Digital Scholarship & Creation at the University of Pittsburgh University Library System. He has advised Elisa on the development of the Digital Mitford project since before it was founded, and he is leading our Project Management workshop.
Zachary Enick is a fourth-year student at the University of Pittsburgh, with a double-major in French and Italian Language studies, a minor in Linguistics, and a certificate in American Sign Language.He has been specializing in digital humanities and applications of the TEI in an advanced manuscript-based translation project on the Lega Toscana of Pittsburgh, PA.
Allyson Hall is a student assistant at Pitt-Greensburg’s Center for the Digital Text who enjoys web design. She has built an XML-based project on Edison and Tesla, and worked with Dorothea Lint on the TEI and design of the Rick and Morty project.
Jonathan Horanic is the senior student assistant at Pitt-Greensburg’s Center for the Digital Text, and he has been working with optical character recognition training over the past year. He is leading our ABBYY Finereader workshop.
Patrick Juola is an internationally noted expert in text analysis, security, forensics, and stylometry, and a professor of computer science at Duquesne University. He is leading the stylometry introduction and workshop.
Dorothea Lint is a Green Scholar research assistant at Pitt-Greensburg who will be helping with Digital Mitford research over the next year. She is the leader of the TEI-based Rick and Morty project which launched this spring.
Rebecca J. Parker is a Digital Studies Fellow at Loyola University in Chicago, and is an alumna of Pitt-Greensburg. She is responsible for introducing GitHub to Pitt-Greensburg’s Digital Studies curriculum, and she is leading our Github training at command line sessions.
